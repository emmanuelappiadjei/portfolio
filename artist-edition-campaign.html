The Artist Edition

A speculative Fender x Ibanez collaboration — a limited-edition instrument collection built around three artists reshaping guitar and bass culture: Thundercat, Steve Lacy, and Toro y Moi.

Role

Creative Concept & Campaign Strategy

Focus

Brand Partnership, Artist Collaboration, Campaign Strategy

Project Type

Speculative Collaboration Concept

The Artist Edition by Emmanuel Appiadjei

Campaign Goals

Build a new generation of guitar icons — transforming instruments associated with Thundercat, Steve Lacy, and Toro y Moi into one collectible, limited-edition line.

  • Reintroduce guitar and bass culture to younger, style-conscious audiences
  • Position Fender and Ibanez at the intersection of music, fashion, and creativity
  • Celebrate artists who challenge traditional genre boundaries
  • Generate demand through scarcity, artist storytelling, and collectible design
  • Inspire fans to move from listening to creating music of their own

Campaign idea: Own the instrument. Carry the legacy. Create what comes next.

Target Audience

Primary: music-driven consumers ages 18–34 who see instruments as both creative tools and cultural objects — fans of alternative R&B, funk, indie, electronic, and experimental music, active across TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Spotify, and drawn to artist collaborations and limited-edition drops.

Secondary: established musicians, collectors, and longtime Fender or Ibanez customers seeking a rare artist-edition instrument.

Audience mindset: they don't just want to listen to the artists who inspire them — they want to create with the same energy.

Why Fender x Ibanez?

Fender represents musical heritage and timeless design; Ibanez represents technical innovation and instruments built for musicians who push sound past traditional limits. Together, the pairing reflects the featured artists — Thundercat's technical skill and fearless experimentation, Steve Lacy's individuality and DIY songwriting, and Toro y Moi's genre-blending production and reinvention. Heritage and innovation, together, make the collection credible to experienced musicians while staying exciting to a younger audience.

Positioning: a meeting point between the instruments that shaped music history and the artists shaping its future.
